As part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy course, I have taken on the role of a **Student Digital Ambassador** at VIT Bhopal. ## Module Summaries

### Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic (Module 1)
Created a one-page visual infographic using **Canva** that explains what digital literacy means to college students. The infographic covers: the definition of digital literacy, essential digital tools for students, safe internet practices, and the importance of a professional online presence. See `task-1-presentation/` for the design.

### Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio (Module 2)
Set up professional profiles on three key platforms:
- **GitHub** – Created a profile README with my name, branch, and learning goals
- **LinkedIn** – Added my education details (VIT Bhopal, Integrated M.Tech AI)
- **Kaggle** – Registered and set up my data science learner profile

Screenshots of all three profiles are in `task-2-portfolio/`.

### Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms (Module 3)
- **Part A (HackerRank):** Completed a beginner coding challenge and earned a badge. Screenshot in `task-3-platforms/`.
- **Part B (Google Forms):** Created a 5-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz with multiple choice and short answer questions, linked to a Google Sheet for response tracking. Screenshot in `task-3-platforms/`.

> 📋 Google Form Quiz Link: https://forms.gle/6bmbQX4ktRedtUfm6

### Task 4 – Professional Email & Etiquette Guide (Module 4)
- Drafted two professional emails: one requesting a deadline extension from a professor, and one applying for a summer internship.
- Created a Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist with 7 items each, focused on responsible use by college students.
- All files are in `task-4-email-etiquette/`.

### Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study (Module 5)
- Wrote a detailed case study on **UPI/Online Payment Fraud**, describing how scammers trick students into authorising payments using fake "collect request" QR codes.
- Created a comprehensive 10-point **Stay Safe Online** prevention checklist, including two UPI-specific tips and reporting resources (cybercrime.gov.in | Helpline: 1930).
- All files are in `task-5-cybercrime/`.
