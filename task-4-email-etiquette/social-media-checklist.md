# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
### Prepared by: Vivek Varma | 25MIM10080 | CSE0001 – Digital Literacy

---

## ✅ DO'S

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a concise bio that reflects your academic or career interests.
2. **Think before you post** – Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?" If not, don't post it.
3. **Use privacy settings wisely** – Regularly review and update your privacy settings on all platforms to control who can see your content.
4. **Engage positively and constructively** – Support peers, share useful resources, and participate in academic or professional discussions.
5. **Verify information before sharing** – Always confirm the credibility of news or claims before resharing, to avoid spreading misinformation.
6. **Build a professional presence** – Use platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub to showcase projects, achievements, and skills.
7. **Report harassment or abuse** – Use in-platform reporting tools if you encounter bullying, hate speech, or inappropriate content.

---

## ❌ DON'TS

1. **Don't share personal details publicly** – Avoid posting your phone number, home address, ID numbers, or daily routine on public profiles.
2. **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying** – Neither participate in nor stay silent about online harassment; report it immediately.
3. **Don't overshare or post impulsively** – Emotional or impulsive posts made in anger or frustration can have long-lasting consequences.
4. **Don't accept requests from strangers carelessly** – Verify unknown profiles before connecting, as fake accounts are commonly used for fraud.
5. **Don't plagiarize or misrepresent others' work** – Always credit original creators and avoid presenting others' content as your own.
6. **Don't use college/institution names in controversial posts** – Your association with VIT Bhopal reflects on the institution; post accordingly.
7. **Don't ignore warning signs of scams** – Avoid clicking on suspicious links, especially in DMs, even from known contacts who may have been hacked.

---

*Remember: Your digital footprint is permanent. What you post today can affect your career tomorrow.*
