# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
### CSE0001 – Digital Literacy | Vivek Varma | 25MIM10080

---

## Overview

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrime categories in India, particularly targeting college students and young adults who frequently use apps like PhonePe, Google Pay, and Paytm.

---

## Case Description (Realistic Scenario)

Aarav, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student, posted on a Facebook group that he was selling his old laptop. A person reached out, claiming to be interested, and sent a QR code saying "scan this to receive ₹15,000." Trusting the buyer, Aarav scanned the code on his UPI app — but instead of receiving money, ₹15,000 was debited from his account.

---

## How It Happened (Step-by-Step)

1. **Targeting** – The fraudster identified Aarav through a public buy/sell post.
2. **Building Trust** – The scammer posed as a genuine buyer and agreed to the price quickly.
3. **The Trap** – A "Collect Request" QR code was sent, disguised as a payment link.
4. **Execution** – Aarav scanned the code without reading the transaction details, authorizing a payment *out* of his account.
5. **Disappearance** – The scammer immediately blocked Aarav and vanished.

---

## Who Is Typically Targeted

- College students and young adults new to digital payments
- People selling items on second-hand marketplaces
- Small business owners unfamiliar with UPI mechanics

---

## Consequences

- Immediate financial loss with little chance of recovery
- Emotional distress, embarrassment, and loss of trust in digital platforms
- In some cases, victims are further harassed for "proof" before they realise the scam
